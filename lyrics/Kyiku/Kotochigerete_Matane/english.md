Wandering the way home without a goal,
Watching days pass like a stranger's tale.
A fleeting hope.

That day, the seawall snatched it all away,
At our forbidden, secret hideaway.
Dim silhouettes swaying in the sea,
With lingering touches still unclear,
I scoop up these fragile, hazy films.
Seasons drift, colors bleed,
Only my heartbeat deafens me,
Drowning out your calling voice.

Friends pass by, walking with strangers now.
*"Don't leave me behind."*
If I could just say it calmly, would it hurt less?
Sinking endlessly, devoid of mass or space.
Leaving only this hollow heart afloat right here.

Swallowing my weakness with a hollow laugh,
Feigning a smile behind an empty joke.
The ground gives way, your outline melts,
If only I could voice this stranded "goodbye."

Screaming in the silence.
So you won't lose your way,
Staring at your transparent back, just like that day.
Hey, even fragile flowers need a reason not to wilt.
Drifting, unable to fade into the background.

Transparent words, and a freezing shell,
Falling embers my numb heart fails to melt.
A fleeting flash, a fading breath... *see you later.*
Yet still, I seek what follows that day's "see you later."

The shoreline, footprints, a stride I cannot gauge.
Though I walk barefoot over splintered wood,
At the bottom of the glass, in your crystal eyes,
What kind of light did I ever reflect?

Beyond the water's edge, a tragic face.
*"It's laughable, isn't it?"*
That frail, distorted silhouette of yours,
Blends into the shore, dissolving in the tide,
Leaving me stranded in the rushing currents.

I was screaming in the silence.
A lost child's echoing cry.
Meeting your transparent gaze, just like that day.
Hey, even fragile flowers die beautifully, right?
Drifting, unable to fade into the background.

Transparent words, and a freezing shell,
Falling embers my numb heart fails to melt.
A fleeting flash, a fading breath... *see you later.*
Yet still, I seek what follows that day's "see you later."
